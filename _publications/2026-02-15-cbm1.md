This paper introduces an innovative non-contact vital signs monitoring system for acupuncture patients, combining multi-channel ballistocardiogram (BCG) signals with multi-task deep learning for simultaneous heart rate estimation, respiration rate estimation, and posture detection.

## Abstract
The study introduces an innovative approach for efficient vital signs monitoring in acupuncture by combining multi-channel ballistocardiogram (BCG) signals and multi-task learning, leveraging the polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) film sensor and deep neural networks. The proposed system utilizes non-contact under-mattress BCG signals for heart rate (HR), respiration rate (RR) estimation and lying posture detection. Integrating Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) and Multi-head Self-Attention (MHSA) mechanisms, the system achieves 98.7% accuracy for activity recognition and 97.6% for lying posture classification. The best MAE for HR achieves 0.77 bpm and for RR achieves 0.43 brpm, compared to an FDA-approved device.

## Key Contributions
- Wireless and non-contact BCG-based system for vital sign monitoring
- Hybrid deep learning model (GRU + MHSA) enabling multi-task detection
- High accuracy across different testing scenarios with 25 participants
- Safety monitoring solution for acupuncture patients without interfering with acupuncture points
